Tributes

Prime Minister Narendra Modi on Thursday paid tributes to A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, saying India can never forget his indelible contribution towards national development, be it as a scientist or the country’s president.

Remembering Bharat Ratna Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam on his jayanti. A visionary leader and architect of India's space and missile programmes, who always wanted to build a strong and self-reliant India. His immortal legacy in the field of science and education is an epitome of inspiration.

The birth anniversary of former President APJ Abdul Kalam on October 15 is observed as World Students' Day.After completing his term as the 11th President of India, Dr Kalam went back to teaching on the very next day. On World Students' Day, we remember how he encouraged students to dream big and never be afraid of failure. Dr Kalam was a role model for teachers also. Good teachers make great human beings, Dr Kalam believed.

The missile man who gave young Indians wings of fire and burning ambition

  • A.P.J. Abdul Kalam was aleader who contributed to the youth skilling and development initiatives.
  • In 2010, the United Nations honoured Kalam for his contribution to science, education and politics. Since then, October 15 is observed as World Students's Day.
  • Four years ago, the Delhi government also rolled out an education scheme called Dr APJ Abdul Kalam Higher Education and Skill Education Guarantee Scheme- Which extends education loans of upto Rs.1million to Students.
  • According to him, the students should acquire two certificates from their school education- the highschool passing certificate and a skill-based achievement certificate.
